# Ethical AI for Long-Term Partner Compatibility

Author: Maksim Nagaev

Conceptual framework exploring how artificial intelligence could support long-term psychological partner compatibility rather than short-term attraction or engagement optimization.

---

## Overview

Most modern relationship technologies optimize for engagement, novelty, and behavioral signals.

This project explores an alternative direction: using AI to help people better understand psychological compatibility before emotional commitment deepens.

Instead of optimizing attraction or user retention, the system would aim to clarify deeper relational structures such as values, emotional regulation, life priorities, and communication patterns.

---

## Core Idea

Modern dating technologies primarily optimize engagement.

This concept explores how AI could instead support deeper psychological compatibility between individuals by combining structured psychological assessment with adaptive AI dialogue.

The goal is not to automate relationship decisions, but to help individuals better understand potential compatibility dynamics.

---

## Proposed System Model

1. **Structured Psychological Assessment**

   Users complete a closed questionnaire designed to capture relatively stable psychological traits, values, and life expectations.

2. **Initial Psychological Profile**

   The responses form a baseline psychological profile.

3. **Adaptive AI Dialogue**

   An AI system engages the user in an adaptive dialogue designed to surface assumptions, emotional reactions, and inconsistencies that static questionnaires often miss.

4. **Psychological Pattern Analysis**

   AI identifies relational patterns across dimensions such as communication style, emotional regulation, and expectations around partnership.

5. **Compatibility Mapping**

   Potential compatibility dynamics can be explored across multiple psychological dimensions.

6. **Compatibility Insight**

   The outcome is not a compatibility score, but a structured understanding of potential alignment or tension areas.

## Key Principles

- AI should support human autonomy rather than replace it
- Psychological compatibility is multidimensional
- Long-term relational stability matters more than engagement metrics
- Technology should clarify decisions, not manipulate emotional dependency

---

## Why This Matters

Many relationship technologies are optimized for attention and emotional engagement.

This raises an ethical question:

Should AI systems designed for human relationships maximize engagement — or support long-term relational wellbeing?

This project explores a conceptual alternative.
